                    <h2>Probability Seminars——Long-term behaviors of stochastic flows of continuous-state branching processes and extremal processes</h2>

                  <div class="capus01">
                    <div class="lecture-line" style="height:80px;">主　题: <span style="color:#8c0000 ; font-family:微软雅黑,Arial,宋体 ;font-weight:bold;">Probability Seminars——Long-term behaviors of stochastic flows of continuous-state branching processes and extremal processes</span><br />
报告人: <span style="color:#333 ; font-family:微软雅黑,Arial,宋体 ;">Prof. Chunhua Ma (Nankai University)</span><br />
时　间: <span style="color:#555 ; font-family:微软雅黑,Arial,宋体 ;">2018-06-04 15:00-16:00</span><br />

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p style="margin: 0px; line-height: 20pt;"><strong><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';color:#000000;margin: 0px; text-transform: none; letter-spacing: 0pt; font-size: 12pt; font-style: normal; font-weight: bold;">Abstract: </span></strong><span style="font-family:'Times New Roman';color:#000000;margin: 0px; text-transform: none; letter-spacing: 0pt; font-size: 12pt; font-style: normal;">A class of stochastic flows of continuous state branching processes were constructed by Le Gall and Bertoin (2000) and Dawson and Li (2012) through subordinators and SDEs respectively. In this work, we study the long-term behaviors of flows of continuous-state branching processes through extremal processes, which arise in the case of supercritical processes with infinite mean and of subcritical processes with infinite variation. The jumps of these extremal processes are interpreted as specific initial individuals whose progenies overwhelm the population. These individuals, which correspond to the records of a certain Poisson point process embedded in the flow, are called super-individuals. They radically increase the growth rate to $+\infty$ in the supercritical case, and slow down the rate of extinction in the subcritical one.</span></p>
